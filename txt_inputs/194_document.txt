© WBCSD / IUCN Dec 2008 Lanka Provincial Government Who you are You represent the interests of the residents of Lanka Province, an area of 1,500 km 2 . These are mostly poor, rural farming communities. A minority of them (~10% = 1,500 people) work for the local Cement and Aggregates Company, while the others mainly live off small-scale farming. You are tasked with the implementation of national regulations (e.g., on wastewater discharge), but suffer from a high level of corruption, due notably to the very low salaries your officials receive. Your budget is currently running at a deficit. Your objectives • Get your budget out of negative figures by the end of the game to avoid being thrown out of power by the national environment minister, Dr. Nate Tchure • Protect and represent the rural community • Alleviate poverty • Create jobs • Decrease vulnerability to food shortages and natural disasters • Improve overall infrastructure • Improve market access • Conserve Lanka Wetlands if possible Potential pathways to achieve objectives • Attract business and investments (create jobs and increase income) • Collecting tax from private sector: o Sweet Home Development Corporation: 200 LaK o Cement and Aggregates Company: 100 LaK o Eco Tour: 50 LaK Game constraints • You cannot enter deals that violate regulation, e.g., wastewater legislation • You cannot sell land, only lease it, i.e., you can only commit to changing land use and management practices • You need to place 10 LaKs in your storm damage fund at the end of the game (after collecting your taxes) “Trump card” You have the power to enforce the new environmental legislation on wastewater discharges, and can collect extra funds from players: for example, if Lanka Cement and Aggregates Company build a treatment plant to treat its wastewater, you can collect the funds. If implemented successfully, additional financial resources are very likely to be allocated by the national government to Lanka Province. Financial status (In debt) Minus – 100 LaK

© WBCSD / IUCN Dec 2008 Sweet Home Development Corporation Who you are You are an international land development company looking to build a high-priced retirement community for Europeans and North Americans in Lanka Province. Your objectives • Secure land (to allow not only for housing, but also significant natural areas for leisure) • Secure building materials needed for construction • Build a world class retirement community • Secure a reliable source of local labor for the construction of the retirement community, as well as the operation of the retirement community • Demonstrate to the provincial government that your business will boost economic development in the region Potential pathways to achieve objectives • Secure development rights in the Lanka Wetlands and surrounding area • Establish good working relationships with local community and industry Game constraints • You are aiming for 5 km 2 of land for construction in and around the Lanka Wetlands, and could potentially offset impacts if you can find suitable land • If you decide to operate in Lanka, you will have to pay 200 LaK in taxes to the Lanka provincial government at the end of the game • To launch your development project, you will need to have a minimum of 950 LaK (after taxes) at the end of the game “Trump card” In the short and long term, constructing the retirement community would generate local employment (tourism, shops, entertainment, restaurants…) . Financial status + 1,500 LaK

© WBCSD / IUCN Dec 2008 Lanka Cement and Aggregates Company Who you are You are a local cement and aggregates company that has been operating in Lanka Province for over 50 years. You employ almost 10% (1,500 people) of the overall population of Lanka Province. Your objectives • Maintain your license to operate, including by complying to environmental regulation about wastewater discharge • Identify a new revenue stream for your rehabilitated quarry that is now a wetland, e.g., through wetland credits, or as land for development or recreation • Build stable demand for your products • Maintain a good relationship with authorities and the local population (supply local labor) Potential pathways to achieve objectives • Building a water treatment plant would guarantee compliance with new wastewater regulation (so far, this is the only way you know of to comply) • Secure endorsements from other stakeholder groups Game constraints • You need to prove compliance with environmental regulations in order to secure license to operate • The cost of building a water treatment plant to treat 100% of your wastewater discharge is 500 LaK, that you would pay to Provincial Government. • If you can secure your license to operate, you will have to pay 100 LaK in taxes to Lanka Provincial Government at the end of the game • You need to have 500 LaK minimum to run your operations (after taxes) “Trump card” You have 5 km 2 of rehabilitated wetland that you own, and therefore can do what you like with. Some of your employees have recently seen the rare Gold-Bearded Hopper (bird) in this rehabilitated quarry. Financial status + 1,000 LaK
