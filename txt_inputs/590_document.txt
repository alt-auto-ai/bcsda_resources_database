FAQs 1 of 3 10 June, 2024 2 What type of visa do I need to attend the conference in Wuhan? You will likely need a business (M) visa. It's recommended to check with the Chinese embassy or consulate in your country for the most current visa requirements. How do I get to Wuhan? Wuhan is well-connected by air, with Wuhan Tianhe International Airport serving as the main gateway. You can book flights directly to Wuhan or connect through other major Chinese cities. How do I get to the conference venue? We offer a complimentary pick-up service. Kindly share your travel itinerary with us so we can coordinate your arrival accordingly. Contact: ren@wbcsd.org Can you recommend other hotels near the conference venue? Yes, we can provide a list upon request. Contact: ren@wbcsd.org Will there be language support at the conference? Yes, the conference will provide English-Mandarin translation services to ensure smooth communication. Are there any additional visits or meetings planned before or after the conference? Absolutely, we can organize extra business visits and meetings as per your requirements. Contact: ren@wbcsd.org

FAQs 2 of 3 10 June, 2024 3 What government representatives from the PRC will be in attendance? This event is organized with the Hubei provincial Government and co-hosted by the Hongtai Group, a state-owned enterprise from Hubei. We expect good representation from the Provincial Government. WBCSD partners with NRDC at the national level, but PRC representation at this first meeting is uncertain. How do we join the High-level lunches? For the accounting platform lunch will priority be given to companies actually listed in Greater China Markets? The aim is to allow discreet interaction between our members and the broader business community in China. Access will depend on seniority, international and Chinese participant balance, and each business's role. WBCSD will aim to distribute access evenly among our members. Lunch participation will be confirmed by June 17.
