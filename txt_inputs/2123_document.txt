Will include the opening and closing. CR1 (864 seats) Ministerial Roundtables Meetings orientated towards Ministerial Dialogue and designed for policymakers as the main audience – even as they feature Non-State Actor speakers and are open to all stakeholders. They could have keynote speakers and/or expert respondents to add value to the discussion. The roundtables will deliberate and suggest high-level strategic messages focusing on shifts needed at the policy level to transform food systems. CR1 (864 seats) High-level Panels Meetings with a full multi-stakeholder orientation, designed to involve practitioners and implementers. Would open with panels with leaders and experts to inform a broad discussion. The high-level panels will focus on capturing the views and key messages from stakeholders – and an inclusive decision-making process – ensuring food systems policies are responsive to real needs. 2 Parallel Events Description Room TBC Featured Event Featured events will cover thematic issues with specific relevance and importance to food systems transformation. Africa Hall or CR2 TBD (458/474 seats) Investment Dialogues Two types of meetings: (1) Discussions that explore issues related to investment in approaches that support a solution-focused agenda around central food system topics: access to safe and nutritious food for all; sustainability; equitable livelihoods; and resilience to vulnerabilities, shocks and stresses. (2) Facilitation of deal-making amongst key stakeholders on prioritized food systems transformation programmes in specific countries, as well as with SMEs. Investment dialogues will deliberate key interventions needed to encourage a positive role for the private sector in food systems transformation. This can, for example, address issues related to the investment environment as well as corporate accountability. Africa Hall (458 seats) Country-led Investment Presentation Up to 10 countries will be provided with space and opportunity to present their concrete investment plans and highlight investible programmes with high potential for engagement from private sector, foundations, development partners, financial institutions, impact funds, etc. These presentations will showcase opportunities that are ready for investment, attracting interest for closer cooperation with partners and help to scale up existing programmes. The goal is to encourage and accelerate increased and targeted investment in food systems and inspire efforts in alignment with the UN Secretary- General’s Statement of Action. CR4 SMEs-led Pitching Sessions Up to 20 SMEs and entrepreneurs are selected by the Business Engagement Group to pitch their ideas and present investment opportunities to the government officials, private sector, investors and partners during moderated sessions to be held on 28 and 29 July 2025.The selected SMEs will come from various regions and demonstrate tangible contributions across the food systems. Their selection will prioritize initiatives that are both impactful and economically viable. To support this process, a digital pitchbook will be developed in the lead-up to the Summit, helping to facilitate participation and provide additional information on key topics for discussion. TBC Monday 28 July and Tuesday 29 July: UNFSS+4 Stocktake The overall programme and flow of the UNFSS+4 Stocktake is guided by the three objectives and priorities highlighted by the UN Secretary-General in his UNFSS+2 Call to Action: • Reflecting on Progress • Partnerships and Tracking Commitments • Unlocking Finance and Increasing Investments The programme begins with a high-level opening session, followed by a plenary dedicated to reflecting on the progress achieved in food systems transformation since the 2021 Food Systems Summit. Over the two-day agenda, each session will focus on a specific thematic food systems enabler or challenge. Topics range from investment and building resilience to harnessing the potential of school meals, climate action, public finance, and scientific innovation.

4 Each of the multistakeholder sessions aims to generate key messages that encapsulate lessons learned and outline actionable steps to sustain momentum. The overarching goal is to emphasize food systems transformation as a critical entry point for achieving the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and to foster continued commitment and collaboration across sectors. The programme, through its thematic focus and inclusive participation of stakeholders, addresses the six action points of the 2023 Secretary-General Call to Action: Incorporating food systems strategies into national policies; establishing inclusive food systems governance; investing in research, data, innovation and technology; deepening joined-up participatory design and implementation; promoting increased engagement of businesses, including through public private partnership and strengthened accountability mechanisms; and ensuring access to short and long-term concessional finance, investments, budget support and debt restructuring. The programme invites all participants, from Heads of State/Government and Ministers to multistakeholder representatives, to collectively reflect on the meaning and power of sustainable food systems amidst a fast changing world, discussing some of the significant progress that has taken place across various regions, and, looking forward, highlighting catalysts of progress and meaningful actions that should inform the direction of a collective effort in the next biennium towards 2030. All sessions will be multistakeholder and informed by the data presented in the UN Secretary-General and Independent Stakeholder Progress Reports. Monday, 28 July Day 1 of the Stocktake will offer space for the highest political leadership to reflect strategically on global developments and their relevance to realizing the right to food globally, unpacking the multifaceted relationships between various drivers and root causes, and reaffirming the necessity to pivot towards more sustainable food systems. Inspiring experiences of impactful policy convergence will be shared by decision makers, while practitioners will reflect in depth on the enablers and barriers of ongoing transitions. A crucial enabler of accelerated efforts at-scale, finance and investments will enjoy heightened focus, structured to operationalize the outcomes of the Financing for Development (FfD4) Conference.
