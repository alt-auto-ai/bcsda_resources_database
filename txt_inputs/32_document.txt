Letter from our CEO About Mars Healthy Planet Thriving People Nourishing Wellbeing Governance Appendix A Letter from the CEO Running a financially fit and a sustainable business are not two mutually exclusive things. In fact, they are self-reinforcing in my opinion. Running a profitable business is the only way we can generate the resources necessary to invest and make a real difference to people, pets, and planet. And when we run a sustainable business, it helps ensure we have a healthy planet where our consumers, clients, and stakeholders thrive, while remaining an attractive place to work, grow, and develop. In 1947, Forrest Mars, Sr. laid down in a letter that the purpose of Mars was to “manufacture and distribute products in such a manner that creates a mutuality of services and benefits among consumers, suppliers, governments, associates, and shareholders.” This principle has endured the test of time, and in 2018 the Mars family established 3 the Mars Compass – a set of shareholder objectives that not only embed our purpose, but also ensure that we make the right choices balancing financial and non-financial targets for generations to come. As I have said many times before, profit and purpose are not enemies. Doing the right thing does not mean a trade-off between planet and productivity, or between environment and employment. To be successful at Mars, we have to deliver financial and non-financial value. Sustainable in a Generation In 2017, we launched the Mars Sustainable in a Generation Plan where we articulated the significant focus and investment Mars had been committing to sustainability for many years. We have now collected progress and action across our full value chain into a comprehensive report. It covers our collective effort to deliver meaningful change while at the same time acknowledging the many challenges ahead of us. The report is organized around the three priority areas we defined back in 2017: Healthy Planet, Thriving People and Nourishing Wellbeing . With regards to Healthy Planet , our climate and environment pillar shows that we have decoupled our business growth from our carbon emissions. Last year, we published our Net Zero Roadmap and promised to further accelerate our carbon reductions with aggressive short- and medium- term targets, but we want to be judged on not what we promise but what we deliver. In 2023, that was a further 8% absolute GHG reduction compared to our 2015 baseline, while growing the business over the $50+ billion mark for the first time. Our GHG reductions are now totaling just over 16%, or 5.7 million metric tons (from a 2015 baseline), across the full value chain. In the same period, our business has grown more than 60%, so the decoupling we want is happening. We still have a long way to go, but we will continue to follow the science and show how a responsible business can both do well and do good. In pursuit of Thriving People , our efforts for workers in our value chain, we have now reached almost 750,000 people through our supplier and origin program since it began in 2017, working diligently on our supply chains. This remains a challenging area for us, as well as many of our peers, and we are dedicating additional resources to deliver scalable progress through partnerships with governments, NGOs, and other stakeholders. 3

Letter from our CEO About Mars Healthy Planet Thriving People Nourishing Wellbeing Governance Appendix To promote Nourishing Wellbeing , our science, innovation, marketing, and nutrition pillar, we have continued our focus to ensure we do the best for our own Associates health, both physically and mentally, as well as how we work to help consumers make healthier choices, and I was pleased to see our Mars Food & Nutrition business deliver 3.1 billion healthy meals – well on its way of delivering 5.5 billion meals by 2025. That progress and the results presented in this report are only possible through our 150,000+ Associates around the world – who make change happen every day. We know that when empowered and motivated, our Associates are our most powerful asset and will deliver innovation and find the solutions that will make change a reality. I am grateful for their contributions, dedication, and resilience, and I am committed to continue to drive positive change in the world through how we do business. The Case for Collective Action There is much to build upon, but I am acutely aware that our progress in isolation will only get us so far. Tackling these enormous challenges requires scale and collective action, driving change across sectors, geographies, and supply chains. This is not easy, and I am not pretending we have all the answers. We are living in complex times, and we have to step up and deliver together in order to achieve a more sustainable future for all. This will require successful businesses that deliver financially and continue to invest in a more sustainable future. And it will require partnership with governments and civil society to ensure the necessary frame- works and abilities to scale collective efforts are in place. We are committed to doing our part. I hope you enjoy reading this report and learning more about our progress, our challenges and how we plan to continue to make a positive difference in the world. Poul Weihrauch CEO & Office of the President, Mars, Incorporated 4
