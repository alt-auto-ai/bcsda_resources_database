PROGRAMME PREVIEW The 3rd SDG Business Forum will take place at the United Nations Headquarters in New York on 17 July 2018. The Business Forum provides a unique multi-stakeholder platform to support business action and partnerships to achieve the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) —fostering public-private dialogues, catalysing new partnerships and alliances, and exploring innovative business solutions to accelerate sustainable development. Each of the sessions at the Business Forum will be tailored to focus on the 2018 High Level Political Forum’s (HLPF) “in focus” goals. 9:15-10:00 OPENING PLENARY Speakers: H.E. Marie Chatardová , President of ECOSOC & Permanent Representative of the Czech Republic to the United Nations (TBC) Mr. Liu Zhenmin , Under Secretary-General for Economic and Social Affairs, United Nations Ms. Lise Kingo , CEO & Executive Director, UN Global Compact Mr. John Denton , Secretary-General, International Chamber of Commerce Keynote address: H.E Ms. Amina J. Mohammed , Deputy Secretary-General, United Nations (TBC) 10:00-11:15 UNLOCKING THE POWER OF BUSINESS AS A FORCE FOR GOOD: MULTI- STAKEHOLDER IMPLEMENTATION OF THE SDG s AND THE ROLE OF BUSINESS An interactive dialogue between business leaders, government ministers, and other stakeholders to showcase how they are collaborating at the local level to advance national SDG priorities. The session will focus how to build effective multi-stakeholder platforms at the country level capable of driving solutions to complex systemic challenges—and to learn from promising approaches. 11:15-11:45 A NEW ERA FOR RESPONSIBLE BUSINESS —A CONVERSATION WITH LEADING CEO s How are leading businesses responding to the challenge of sustainability? 11:45-13:00 LEADERSHIP & INNOVATION FOR THE SDGS This session will examine innovative and leading solutions for the SDGs in review during the 2018 HLPF. How can policy reforms enable greater action by the private sector and where the UN system can add greatest value? Are the SDGs providing a new framework for innovation? And how are businesses integrating the SDGs as a framework for future growth? 13:15-14:45 HIGH-LEVEL LUNCH* 15:00-17:00 Parallel session I SUSTAINABLE CITIES Many SDGs under review this year have an urban focus. Local authorities struggle with raising money for sustainable investments and also wonder how they can make it more attractive for business to invest. What are the solutions? Parallel session II MAKING THE SDG s EVERYONE’S BUSINESS How can the engagement of small businesses on the SDGs be enhanced? A discussion involving multinational corporations, medium sized firms, co-operative, and small-scale businesses to examine the different challenges they face in implementing the SDGs. Parallel session III POLICY DIALOGUE FOR THE SDG s Examples of collective action with a focus on the SDGs in review. How is the private sector contributing to national Voluntary National Review processes —and what’s the impact? How can business work with governments to develop policy frameworks and partnerships that drive sustainable development? 17:45-19:15 RECEPTION* * By invitation only.
