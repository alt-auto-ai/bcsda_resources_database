WBCSD Antitrust Compliance Policy v. 2.0 Approved AGM 4 December 2025 Our Commitment to Fair Competition WBCSD is committed to maintaining the highest standards of competition law compliance. Throughout all aspects of our mission, we are dedicated to ensuring that all decisions, interactions, and policies reflect a steadfast respect for the principles of fair and open competition. Our commitment to integrity, transparency, and ethical conduct is central to how we operate. This Antitrust Policy affirms our dedication to complying with all applicable competition laws and provides clear guidance and expectations to our staff, members, and stakeholders on navigating situations that may carry antitrust risks. Upholding the principles of fair competition is not only a legal obligation—it is a core value that shapes our culture and guides our behavior across every level of the organization. Beyond compliance, it serves as a driving force behind the sustainability goals WBCSD aims to achieve. These goals must be anchored in fair competition, which stands as a cornerstone of meaningful progress and responsible transformation in the global economy.

WBCSD Antitrust Compliance Policy v. 2.0 Approved AGM 4 December 2025 1. Introduction Antitrust laws are designed to promote and maintain free and fair competition amongst market participants, by prohibiting certain behaviors such as Agreements or Concerted practices that may impact or restrict competition. WBCSD, as an association which works with Member companies from a wide range of industries and convenes them to collaborate on programs, projects, and Events aimed at advancing sustainability within their respective businesses and sectors, recognizes that such collaboration may result in close interaction between Members, who may be Competitors in their individual markets. WBCSD and its Members are strongly committed to conducting their activities in a manner consistent with all principles of fair competition. This shared commitment reinforces WBCSD’s reputation for ethical conduct and contributes to the advancement of the Members’ sustainability goals. Therefore, WBCSD and its Members are firmly dedicated to carrying out their activities in compliance with all applicable antitrust laws. In particular, it is paramount for the integrity of WBCSD’s activities that they do not and cannot become, even inadvertently, a forum for prohibited Agreements, Concerted practices, discussions or behaviors which could have an illicit impact on competition in the Members’ respective markets.

WBCSD Antitrust Compliance Policy v. 2.0 Approved AGM 4 December 2025 2. Definitions To ensure clarity and consistency in the interpretation of this Antitrust Policy, the following key terms are defined below. Unless expressly stated otherwise, each term shall be understood to include both its singular and plural forms. a. Abuse of dominant position means a situation in which a company holding a dominant position in a relevant market exploits that position in a way that restricts competition or harms consumers or Competitors. b. Agreement means any form of formal or informal understanding, arrangement or alignment of conducts, with or without binding force, between companies operating at the same or different market levels, that have the object or effect of restricting competition. c. Antitrust Moderator has the meaning set forth in Article 11. d. Antitrust Policy or Policy refers to this Antitrust Compliance Policy, as amended from time to time. e. Antitrust Statement refers to the document attached hereto as Appendix 1. f. Benchmarking means a structured comparison of other companies’ ideas, processes, practices or methods, with the intention of identifying strengths, weaknesses or best practices and transferring them to one’s own business to operate more efficiently. g. Cartel means an Agreement between Competitors or business partners that restricts elements of free competition, such as pricing, production, or market sharing. h. Commercially Sensitive Information has the meaning set out in Article 6.3. i. Competitor means a company active on the same relevant market as another company. j. Concerted practice refers to a form of coordination between companies that, without reaching the stage of a formal agreement, involves a conscious and voluntary alignment of conduct, knowingly resulting in behavior that infringes antitrust laws. k. Events refers to any event operated, organized, sponsored or held by or on behalf of WBCSD including, but without limitation, a conference, exhibition, convention, congress, summit, seminar, webinar, meeting, workshop, dinner, public training and private or bespoke training, shows, whether in person, online, or in whatever format. l. Horizontal agreement means an Agreement or Concerted practice between two or more economically independent Competitors operating at the same level of the market. These companies are at the same market level when they are actually or potentially in competition with each other due to the substitutability of their goods and services.
