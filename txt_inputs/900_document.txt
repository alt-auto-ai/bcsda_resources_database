WBCSD Leadership Program | 2 Our world faces three critical challenges: a climate emergency, nature in crisis and mounting inequality. In 2021, we launched Vision 2050 , a framework for business action in line with the urgency of the challenges that we face if 9+ billion people are to live well, within planetary boundaries, by 2050. To realize this vision, WBCSD is committed to supporting leaders across different business functions and industries in incorporating resilience into strategic planning, building a regenerative approach to business models, and reorienting global systems of capital towards sustainable development. We have incorporated this approach into WBCSD’s Leadership Program, which, for the last 20 years, has supported business leaders in navigating the global sustainability landscape to implement long- Prof Dr Rodney Irwin Chief Operating Officer Forewords term, resilient business strategy and operations. I have had the privilege of overseeing the program’s development for the past nine years, and I am always inspired by the innovative ideas, concrete solutions, and lasting relationships from the impassioned, fearless business leaders who participate. We will continue leveraging our networks and knowledge expertise to guide participants through this year’s curriculum. Participants will take part in briefings with leading experts as well as experiential and peer-to-peer learning to gain cutting-edge sustainability insights and apply them into action in their own companies. WBCSD is proud to deliver this curriculum in partnership with Yale University, as well as in collaboration with our member companies, who represent the world’s most forward-thinking businesses driving accelerated progress in the sustainability agenda. Business resilience requires sustainability leadership to come from all business functions. We welcome you to join a dynamic community of business leaders committed to realizing a world where everyone can live healthy, fulfilling lives within planetary boundaries. We look forward to meeting you in this learning journey and working with you to translate your ideas into action. I like to say that the WBCSD Leadership Program is a unique opportunity to become a sustainable development leader. It’s a learning program mixing lectures by professors, experiential learning activities, discussion with business experts and experience sharing sessions with peers. Each year participants not only increase their knowledge on sustainable development challenges and opportunities but they also learn about supporting tools and frameworks. At the end of the Program, participants have a deeper understanding of their role and purpose to add value to their organization: they can make actionable recommendations to their companies. They develop leadership skills and a supportive network of leaders. I believe, those are some of the reasons why Alumni do recommend the Program. The Leadership Program will continue to evolve but we have decided to keep an overall focus and core curriculum on sustainability strategy and actions instead of changing the topic every year. After two challenging years, I certainly hope to once again to meet face-to-face with the Leadership Program participants in the Class of 2022 and look forward to receiving nominations – don’t hesitate to reach out with questions! WBCSD Leadership Program | 3 Why join the WBCSD Leadership Program? Education is central to improving the lives of people globally. The 2030 development agenda recognizes that education is essential for the success of sustainable development as a whole with Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) 4, which focuses specifically on education. The WBCSD Leadership Program helps representatives from companies gain an in-depth understanding of sustainable development challenges and the opportunities that feed into strategic business decision-making. As the world continues to change, sustainable businesses will grow to be the most successful. Therefore, it’s critical for the next generation of leaders to understand the importance of sustainability and integrate related challenges and opportunities into their core business strategy. The Program is designed to help business leaders navigate complex topics and concepts that will determine the future, enabling individuals and organizations to lead, transform and succeed. Many companies say they’re integrating the SDGs into their corporate strategy. When developing such a strategy, innovation is key for futureproofing and adapting business models and/or product service offerings. In other words, challenging our thinking is critical for building a sustainable world. The WBCSD Leadership Program explores how companies are integrating human and social capital into their core business strategies and day-to-day decision-making. It helps participants understand how cutting-edge companies use innovation to develop value creating solutions that address sustainability issues and opportunities. Participants will participate in panel discussions with academics and business leaders, investors and NGOs, as well as onsite visits and practical workshops.

WBCSD Leadership Program | 4 What participants gain by joining Enhance leadership skills: Participants gain a better understanding of their own leadership preferences to help them become sustainability leaders. Partnership with Universities: We start our Program at IMD followed by Yale University with world-renowned academics. After successful completion of the Program, participants receive a certificate from IMD and Yale School of Management. Yale professors who have contributed in previous years include Prof Rodrigo Canales, Prof Zoë Chance, Prof Dan Esty and Prof Brad Gentry. Build a strong network: The Program creates a global network of dynamic business leaders, capable of acting as sustainable development ambassadors within their companies and in society. There are also opportunities to engage with a range of business and sustainability experts. Previous speakers include Paul Polman, Peter Bakker (WBCSD), Samuel DiPiazza (PWC/Citi), Lord Michael Hastings, Prof Mervyn King (IIRC), Karim Hajjar (Solvay), Nadir Godrej (Godrej Industries) and S. Padmanabhan (Tata). Strengthen business skills: Participants work on individual and group projects focusing on solutions that benefit the participant’s company and the wider business community. Final group projects are presented during WBCSD’s Council Meeting, where participants interact with WBCSD members at the C-Suite level. Varied experiences: Highly interactive, the Program uses a mixture of education styles from traditional classroom-based work to action and experiential learning activities through workshop participation, case studies and presentations to help candidates to succeed in their career. Long-term education: Participants are eligible apply for credits at Lancaster University based on their learning on the WBCSD Leadership Program. To find out more, email Dr Alison Stowell for details.
