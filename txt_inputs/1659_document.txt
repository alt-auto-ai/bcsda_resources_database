Global Game-Changers for better food systems The Private Sector Guiding Group convened by WBCSD is actively participating in the efforts to accelerate food systems transformation by supporting key initiatives around the world that could be defined as ‘Game-Changers’. These ‘Game-Changers’ are examples of existing and planned actions for better food systems across the UN Food Systems Summit Action Tracks through innovation, partnerships and community empowerment. Geographic spread The Game-Changers are helping lead the transformation of food systems at the international level and are looking for more opportunities to connect and learn from others.* Emissions These Game- Changers collectively decreased over 96 million tons of CO₂ related emissions through carbon sequestration methods and behavioral change at the consumer and retail levels.* Land regenerated The Game-Changers have positively transformed 169 million hectares of land for sustainable farming through using regenerative agriculture practices.* Market opportunity The Game-Changers are globally operating in a growing market, the Growing Better report from the Food and Land Use Coalition estimates that the business opportunity of food systems transformation is worth 4.5 trillion USD per year by 2030 .* People impacted Across the entire value chain , the Game-Changers are positively impacting over 1.18 billion people through education, employment, food security, youth and gender empowerment.* Innovation & Leadership Over 88 Game-Changers are leveraging their public-private partnerships to support innovation and leadership towards food system transformation at the local, regional and global levels.* Action Tracks All Game-Changers are demonstrating progress across two or more action tracks.* 5 11 14 3 11 2 18 42 with global reach 88 innovation & leadership initiatives SMEs Over 36 Game-Changers are focusing on strengthening the resilience of SMEs and smallholder farmers through education, policy-framing and public-private partnerships.* 169m ha regenerated 96m tons of CO₂ 1: Ensuring access to safe and nutritious food 58 2: Shifting to sustainable consumption patterns 39 3: Boosting nature-positive production 56 4: Advancing equitable livelihoods 47 5: Building resilience 47 Initiatives 36 1.18bn people benefit 4.5tn per year * These numbers and examples are taken from a WBCSD-led online survey, based on 94 individual submissions.
